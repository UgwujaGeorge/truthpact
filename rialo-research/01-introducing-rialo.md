**Title:** Introducing Rialo: A Blockchain Built for the Real World
**Published:** September 25, 2025
**URL:** https://rialo.io/posts/introducing-rialo

## One-line
Rialo's positioning manifesto — a "real-world blockchain": a decentralized, high-performance, ergonomic programming network that interacts directly with real life.

## Core claims
- Web3 has chased TPS while losing the user. Real adoption requires Web3 to come *to* Web2 (not the other way around).
- Today's chains are "blind to real-world events" — they cannot directly interact with off-chain services without bolted-on middleware.
- Rialo is "supermodular by design": each capability amplifies the rest.
- Analogy used throughout: smartphones combined GPS + camera + connectivity → unlocked Uber/Instagram/WhatsApp. Rialo claims to do the same convergence for blockchain.

## The eleven "Real World …" capabilities (Rialo's named primitives)
1. **Real World Data** — pull live data with a one-line HTTPS call inside a smart contract.
2. **Real World Connectivity** — seamless integration with off-chain APIs in smart contracts.
3. **Real World Privacy** — fast/affordable confidential + verifiable execution.
4. **Real World Identity** — email/SMS/social as Web3 passport; credit-score-based crypto loans; smart-contract wills.
5. **Real World Speed** — true sub-second e2e latency for users.
6. **Real World Scalability** — Multiple Concurrent Proposers (MCP) for global scale.
7. **Real World Reactivity** — on-chain reactions without off-chain monitoring/gas wars.
8. **Real World Programmability** — `Future`/`Promise`/`.await`, event-driven logic, randomness; "smart contracts that sleep, loop, resume." Powered by RISC-V.
9. **Real World Usability** — 2FA, scheduled txs, social logins.
10. **Real World Assets** — mainstream and long-tail RWAs.
11. **Real World Applications** — apps people use daily, beyond speculation.

## Pain points cited (good "wedge map" for product ideas)
- Users: cross-chain interop friction, wallet UX, gas spikes, sandwich attacks, oracle-glitch liquidations, rebuilding social identity, 3 AM token drops, irreversible wallet hacks.
- Devs: drowning in integrations (10% time on business logic), slow reads despite fast finality, fragile keepers/cron jobs, expensive indexers ("$thousands/mo to cloud providers"), oracle tax, bridges as risk vectors.
