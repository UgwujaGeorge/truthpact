# Source: Subzero Labs Welcomes Cryptographer Jan Camenisch

**Published:** December 19, 2025
**URL:** https://rialo.io/posts/rialo-welcomes-jan-camenisch

## One-line
Camenisch joining Subzero is a strong tell about Rialo's privacy + identity ambitions. He literally co-invented anonymous credentials.

## Why he matters
- Co-designed **Identity Mixer (Idemix)** — the canonical anonymous-credential system. Selective disclosure ("prove you're over 18 without showing your birthday") and unlinkable credentials.
- Co-invented **CL signatures** — foundation for ZK-friendly signature/credential schemes used widely in decentralized identity.
- 30,000+ paper citations, 70+ US patents, 2024 Levchin Prize, ACM Fellow.
- Founded post-quantum research team at IBM Zurich; some of those algorithms became NIST PQC standards.
- Former CTO of Dfinity — led NIDKG (rotating subnet keys), threshold signatures, secure randomness, scalable distributed compute architecture.

## Direct implications for what's buildable on Rialo
- Anonymous credentials → KYC'd-but-private apps (regulated DeFi, voting, age-gated services).
- Threshold signatures + TEEs → user wallets that don't have a single seed phrase failure mode (matches the "2FA / familiar logins" pitch in the intro post).
- Post-quantum cryptography on the roadmap.
- The privacy stack (REX + MPC + FHE + TEEs) is being engineered by someone who has shipped this in production at IBM and Dfinity, not a Web3-only team.

## Quote from Camenisch
"Privacy and verifiable automation are essential for Web3 to become part of the wider digital economy. Rialo's architecture is grounded in strong cryptography and is exactly what's needed to connect Web3 and Web2 securely."
